Local Outreach
encourages reaching out to your neighbor.

Evangelism

ALCF’s Evangelism ministry believes that sharing our faith is an integral part of our life in Christ. Altar Prayer Team members come alongside individuals who commit to following Jesus Christ or are re-dedicating their lives at the end of each worship service. Our goal is to help them find the spiritual support and fellowship they need to grow in Christ, and to equip them to reach out to others with the Good News that they have embraced for themselves.

For detailed information about becoming an Altar Prayer Team member, click here.

Contact: Pastor Tobey Hall | evangelism@alcf.net


Outreach

Our outreach ministries are focused on taking the Good News of Jesus Christ into communities in need including jails, prisons, juvenile halls, elder care homes, public schools, and to the homeless living out on the streets and in shelters. Our efforts also include partnership assistance with local organizations and help with community service projects.

Contact: Pastor Tobey Hall | outreach@alcf.net

The Bridge

"The Bridge" is ALCF’s monthly recovery event that takes place on first Saturdays. A night of lively music and sharing with a focused message about recovery, this inspiring event is attended by 100+ people. Refreshments and childcare provided.

Homeless

ALCF's Homeless Ministry strives to encourage those in despair and convey hope through acts of service that exemplify Christ's unconditional love, proclaim the saving message of the Gospel and welcome those in need of finding their true home in Christ.

View details »

Prison (AfterCare)

ALCF’s Jail, Juvenile Hall and Prison Ministry is a valuable asset to the local community. By encouraging inmates in local correctional facilities through the Word, worship and prayer, our ministry teams have seen the lives of men, women and youth fully surrendered to our Savior and Lord, Jesus Christ.

View details »

Elder Care

Over 60% of nursing home residents do not receive regular visits from family and friends. Through ALCF’s ElderCare ministry, we have an opportunity to pray and fellowship with seniors at nursing care facilities who may feel forgotten or are unable to attend a regular church service. Training requirements vary based on your chosen role and facility site.

View details »

Angel Tree

Angel Tree is a program of Prison Fellowship. ALCF partners with Angel Tree to reach out with the love of Christ to the children and families of prisoners. This unique ministry gives the entire ALCF community an opportunity to participate by helping to meet their physical, emotional, and spiritual needs during the Christmas season through gifts, church services and special programs.

View details »

Kids Club

ALCF's Kids Club is a biblically based program offered at Brentwood Academy, a public elementary school in East Palo Alto. This free, after school program is designed to help students in what they most often lack: a consistent, strong role model and mentor who is willing to spend time with them so they can be helped academically, socially, spiritually and emotionally. The goal is not to just teach them biblical lessons and values, but to help them apply God’s Word to situations and circumstances in their own lives given the challenges they face at home, in their neighborhoods and at school.

View details »
