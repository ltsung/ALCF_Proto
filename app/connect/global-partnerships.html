Global Partnerships

Jesus commands us to go and make disciples of all nations (Matthew 28:18–30). Our hope is that we would faithfully live out that commandment both as individuals and as a church body, with the assurance that He is always with us and desires to work through us as we walk in obedience. We support the work of God around the world through various international partnerships, both financially and through prayer.

Chandra Chap

Chandra Chapp, a native Cambodian, is the Director of Pleroma Home for Girls. They exist to create a safe haven for girls under 18, who have been victims—or are at high risk for being victims—of human trafficking. They are nurtured holistically to realize their full potential as God’s precious daughters with His glorious image restored in them. Chandra’s work includes counseling girls, overseeing and training dorm moms, teachers, social workers, and counselors, travelling several times per month to distant provinces for court days and family follow up, as well as networking with providers throughout Cambodia.

John Gibbs

John Gibbs, a Stanford graduate and former Apple employee, is helping equip churches with high-tech evangelism and outreach in the hopes of reaching many Japanese through these mediums. John is currently working with Japanese Christians to develop a mobile phone Japanese Anime version of the Bible to spread the Gospel among the technology-savvy people of Japan.

The Thomspons

Women in the sex industry all have a story of exploitation (e.g. poverty, rape, gender inequality, lack of education, family greed, or trauma) making them desperate enough to sell their bodies for survival.
